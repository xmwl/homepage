Raise Marketplace

Product Designer (July 2015 - August 2016)

I joined Raise in July 2015 as a Product Designer when Raise acquired Tastebud Technologies. My work was on Raise’s iOS app, Android app, and Raise.com. My role focused on feature enhancements, product iteration, and maintaining the Raise style throughout its consumer-facing products.

Below is a small selection of the projects I've worked on.

Brand Screens for iOS and Android
Product, Design, Prototyping

The old brand screens in the Raise iOS and Android app presented many challenges in the buy flow process for a customer. The main problems to solve with the new design for the brand screens were around the organization of brand information and issues of space, in particular, on smaller screen sizes.

I focused on updating the merchant specific section on the top-half of screen, allowing the merchant information to be hidden when scrolling through a list of available cards.

Mobile Wallet for iOS and Android
Product, Design, Prototyping

The goal of this project was to create a wallet redemption design that allowed Raise customers to better understand and manage their gift cards, while also creating an improved experience for cashiers redeeming gift cards bought from Raise.

I led the design for the new wallet redemption and worked with the UX team to get real feedback to drive the design process.

The wallet redemption design featured better branding for each individual brand, easier to read card information, a new way to isolate the most important card information when checking out.

Tab Navigation for iOS
Product, Design, Prototyping

I led the design efforts to move the Raise iOS app from a hamburger menu to a tabbed navigation. I worked with the engineers and mobile PM to determine the focus of app and which features needed to be placed within the tab navigation.

I was responsible for designing the new More section and updating any previous interactions within the app that conflicted with the new tab navigation. I also created a prototype of the new app for user testing.

Prototypes built with Origami and Invision.

Sell A Card on iOS
Design, Prototyping

The ability to sell unwanted gift cards has been an important feature of the Raise iOS app since the app’s launch. It had never been updated even though many parts of the app had changed around it.

The goal of this project was to update the design of the sell flow within the app so that it matched the improved styles and standards of the rest of the app.

Receipts for Android
Design

The goal is this project was to add the ability for Raise users to view receipts for their purchased gift cards in the Android app.

Buy Flow for iOS and Android
Product, Design, Prototyping, Research

The goal of this project was to improve the buy flow for the apps, in particular, giving customers greater ability to explore different brands through categories and better overview of cards in limited quantity.

Tastebud Technologies (Formerly StyleSeek)

Product Designer, Front-End Developer, Content Manager (May 2012 - July 2015)

As the company grew, I took on the QA role for both StyleSeek and Tastebud products and eventually became the front-end developer and product designer, in charge of all the design efforts for web and mobile at the company.

NEX Outlets for iOS
Product, Design, Prototyping, Research, QA

I led the design for the version 3.0 overhaul of the NEX Outlets iOS app.

The goal of the project was to create a coherent experience that fit within the iOS ecosystem and met the business objectives of Tastebud and NEX Outlets.

I worked with two mobile engineers and a product manager to successfully redesign the app (both visually and on the backend) in addition to creating a new Rewards Program for the mall within the app.

NEX Outlets for Android

Similar to the iOS app, I led the design in the building of the first native Android app for NEX Outlets.

Personal Projects

Did France Win?

2016

As a dual French and American citizen, and someone whose identity is firmly split between the two countries, my support for the French national football has never wavered. I always want to know the latest score, but never want to have to fumble around in a larger sports app to find the simple score I need. The result: the Did France Win? app.

I used the project as starting point to teach myself Swift. While I’m familiar with Xcode and have made design tweaks in code and Interface Builder in the past, I never truly built my own app from scratch until now.

The app is available now in the App Store.
